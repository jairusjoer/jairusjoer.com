In previous posts, I wrote about my philosophy on software personalisation, as well as my recent work on the subject at HERO Software. In this post, I will continue this exploration by demonstrating examples of software personalisation on the web in the context of theming.

Theming, in essence, is the extension of an identity into software. Whether that identity is that of a company or an individual is indifferent to the purpose of this extension: association.

In software, associations can be formed through a variety of features. In its simplest form, it may offer the option to upload a custom logo; in its most advanced form, it is designed to respect interconnected user preferences.

Colour is a favourable option for enabling association, from their application in user interfaces to their systematisation in multi-brand design systems. To explore the growing complexity of colour and the associated considerations, choose a colour:

<ThemeColor client:load />

A single colour may not appear particularly complex in isolation, but its complexity becomes apparent when it interacts with the interface. When control of a colour is handed over to the user, the amount of considerations increases in line with the size of the colour palette available.

Contrast must be considered as it is a <abbr title="Web Content Accessibility Guidelines">WCAG</abbr> requirement. In the context of a fixed palette, this can be resolved by providing hardcoded contrast colours. However, this quickly becomes impractical when a colour space is offered through a picker.

Previously, JavaScript libraries were necessary to reliably calculate the optimal contrast colour based on a theme colour. However, in April 2026, the CSS [`contrast-color()`](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/CSS/Reference/Values/color_value/contrast-color) method became widely available and aimes to solve this issue:

<ThemeContrast client:load />

`contrast-color()` ensures minimum contrast in accordance with [WCAG AA standards](https://w3c.github.io/wcag/guidelines/22/#contrast-minimum) and returns an appropriately contrasting colour of either black or white based on the colour provided. In this demonstration, the theme colour picker sets the CSS variable `--color-accent`, which can be applied as follows:

```css
:root {
  --color-accent-contrast: contrast-color(var(--color-accent));
}
```
